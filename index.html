While you are here, there is still hope

Understanding the problem is the first and the most important step towards solving it.

  • Garbage and waste

    A survey conducted in all 58 municipalities of Nepal in 2012 found that the average municipal solid waste generation was 317 grams per capita per day. This translates into 1,435 tons per day or 524,000 tons per year of municipal solid waste generation in Nepal.

  • Pot holes and broken roads

    Broken roads and pot holes are one of the major causes of accidents. Kathmandu alone had 4,770 accidents in the year 2012-13 which caused 148 deaths. As of 2015 we have 80,000km of road, many of which have problems in them.

  • Other problems

    Being a developing nation. It goes without saying that we have a lot of problems in the community. Including different kinds of pollution and other problems that are likely to be not noticed by the authorities. Do let us know and we shall add them to the app.
